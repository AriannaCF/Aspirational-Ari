    I'm Not Lazy, It's Called a Capsule Wardrobe and it's Classy

    June 9th, 2022 by Arianna

    A Closet Full of Nothing to Wear

    During my years working at Nordstrom, I accumulated quite the collection of clothing. Between giveaways, contest prizes, and the allure of my employee discount, I was coming home with a shopping bag almost daily at one point, and by the time I left my career there, my closet was bursting at the seams. Despite, or maybe because of this, the vast majority of the time when I opened my closet door to get dressed for the day, I almost always had nothing to wear. Usually after hours of searching, I would usually settle for one of a few outfits that I felt comfortable and confident in and tended to default to. If I had to estimate, I only wore about 30% of my pieces on a regular basis, while the vast majority of my closet sat untouched.

    Once I left fashion retail and had access to neither the employee discount nor the daily temptation of in-store marketing, my shopping habits changed quite a bit. I still had my massive closet, and still cycled through the same dozen or so outfits I felt most comfortable in. Then, one day while browsing Pinterest, I came across the concept of a capsule wardrobe.

    Introducing: The Capsule Wardrobe

    According to Sustainably Chic, “A capsule wardrobe is a limited selection of interchangeable clothing pieces that complement each other.” They usually are composed of about 30 basic pieces, including a two or three tanks tops, tee shirts, blouses, skirts, jeans, slacks, and dresses along with one or two sweaters, jackets, a few pairs of shoes and several accessories to tie it all together. The purpose of a capsule wardrobe is to make it easier to get dressed in the morning since you only have a limited selection of curated pieces that all coordinate with one another.

    Once I discovered the capsule wardrobe, I basically had my work cut out for me. I already had most of my capsule collected, it was just a matter of cutting out the excess. Over the course of the next few weeks, I trimmed the fat of my closet, donating almost three full bags of clothes to goodwill and even selling a few pieces on eBay. At first I kept two capsules, one for the colder fall and winter months, and another for spring and summer. Since moving to Florida however, I only use the one, though I do keep my winter capsule on hand for the few days of the year when it does get a little chilly. Ultimately I'm grateful for the wardrobe capsule, as it allows me to do what I was doing anyway without the stress of having a closet full of nothing to wear. And more importantly, my bank account is also much happier!
