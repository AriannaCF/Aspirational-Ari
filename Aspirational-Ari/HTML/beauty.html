No Makeup Makeup - How to Catfish With Your Makeup Routine

June 4th, 2022 by Arianna

My Humble Beginnings

I'll never forget the day I discovered the transformational power of makeup. I was a sophomore in high school, sitting at a table in the cafeteria with my friends during free period talking about whatever teenage girls talked about in 2006. One of my friends was sitting right across from me, putting on her makeup while we chatted, and I got to watch the process from start to finish. It was a very simple routine, just some light powder and blush, eyeliner and a touch of mascara. She was only about 16 years old, so she didn't need any of it of course, but the before and after was striking.

This was a little bit before the era of instagram and then endless sea of YouTube tutorials we have now, so up to that point I had only ever watched my mom's makeup routine, and had never seen a similar transformation on a girl my own age. The most makeup I had ever worn up to that point was for a halloween costume when I was maybe seven or eight years old, for which my mom diligently and expertly recreated 1920's style flapper girl makeup look, complete with Betty Boop lipstick and razor thin brows. Outside of costume makeup and some neutral lipstick on special occasions, I hadn't really worn much makeup, and had never applied it myself.

That same day after school, I stopped my the Walmart on my way home to pick up some supplies. The first makeup I ever bought was black eyeliner, mascara, and pink lip gloss. I went home and started practicing immediately. My first attempt looked insane, I want way to heavy on the eyeliner and it was super uneven. After about three times of washing it off and reapplying, I finally figured out the perfect amount to apply. A simple thin line right on the lower eyeliner and mascara on the upper lid to balance it out was the best I could do. From then on that was my daily makeup routine.

Later my mom taught me about powder foundation, and gave the one of her Maybelline eyebrow pencils to fill in my brows, a product that I still use to this day over 15 years later. While I've varied my makeup routine through the years, my go to daily look in recent years has been a much more natural look that omits eyeliner altogether and relies mostly on good skincare and bone structure.

The No Makeup Makeup Routine

It's exactly what it sounds like. The goal is to do your makeup in such a way that it looks like you're not wearing any makeup at all. I'm not even going to front, the ultimate goal is to deceive. I want to be able to loudly proclaim “I Woke Up Like This” while lying through my teeth and not being questioned at all. I have two main methods of pulling off this proverbial slight of hand. The first was taught to me my the one and only Jackie Aina.

The second method is a bit more heavy handed, and meant for times when I want to look casual and effortlessly gorgeous without needing to necessarily trick anyone into thinking I have a bare face. Those uninitiated to makeup (and pretty much all men, let's be real) can probably be fooled into thinking that this is my natural face, while anyone who wears makeup will most likely not be fooled. This particular method was taught to me by the lovely Tea Renee.

And that's it! That's how I trick people into thinking that I look way better than I actually do. At times do I feel guilty for using the tools of the feminine trade to deceive the masses? Sure, but then I just remember all of the men out there who are concealing their weak chins and nonexistent jawlines with full, luscious beards, and I immediately feel vindicated.
